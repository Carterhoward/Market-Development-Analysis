Primary finding

Bid counts and active sub % falling together is the clearest pre-collapse signal

When both active sub % and total bid counts are declining simultaneously in the 3 months before a break, retention collapses in 53% of events. No single metric predicts collapse reliably — but when two or more warning signals fire at the same time, the probability rises sharply. Kansas City, Atlanta, Chicago, Dallas, and Miami all showed this dual-decline pattern before their breaks.

Important nuance

GC posting rate alone is not a reliable collapse predictor — but its drop below 15% is a warning

GC posting rate fell in only 31% of collapses — meaning it often stays stable even as retention is about to break. However, in the major collapses (drop ≥10pp), GC posting rate below 15% was present in all cases at or near the break point. It's a floor condition: markets that stay above 19% rarely collapse. Markets that drift to 13–15% are vulnerable when the other signals also weaken.

Silver lining

Collapses are recoverable — Pittsburgh and Louisville both bounced back

Pittsburgh broke below 75% in August 2024 (69% trough) and recovered to 76% by March 2026. Louisville dipped and recovered twice. The recovery pattern in both cases preceded a GC posting rate rise above 19% by approximately 3–4 months. The same leading indicator that predicts collapse, when reversed, predicts recovery.

04

The warning playbook

Based on the pre-collapse pattern analysis, here's the monitoring framework and intervention timing for each warning stage.

01

Watch: Two metrics softening

Active sub % and bid counts both declining month-over-month but still above baseline. GC posting rate holding above 17%. Retention still above 78%.

Action: Flag for monitoring. Review GC posting incentives.
02

Warning: Three metrics declining

Active sub %, bid counts, AND unique bidders all trending negative for 2+ months. GC posting rate drops to 15–17%. Retention still above 75% but compressing.

Action: Activate GC posting campaign. Investigate why subs are disengaging.
03

Critical: GC posting below 15% + dual decline

GC posting rate falls below 15% while active sub % and bid counts are both declining. Retention likely still above 75% but this combination has preceded every major collapse (≥10pp) in the dataset.

Action: Emergency intervention. Subs are about to stop renewing in 1–2 cycles.
04

Break: Retention crosses below 75%

Retention drops below 75% for the first time after a streak. The warning window has closed. Recovery is possible (Pittsburgh: 6 months, Louisville: 4 months) but requires GC posting rate to recover above 19% first.

Action: Recovery mode. Focus entirely on GC activation, not sub acquisition.
Key timing insight

3 months is the minimum warning window — 6 months is when signals first appear

The pre-collapse signals appear on average 6 months before the retention break, but become strongest (and most reliable) in the 3-month window immediately before. This means a monthly monitoring cadence is essential — quarterly reviews would miss the warning window entirely for fast-moving collapses like Kansas City.

Raleigh broke to 74% after a 22-month streak in February 2026. Nashville holds at 72% but active sub % has been declining. Both have GC posting rates above the 19% threshold (Raleigh 21%, Nashville 20%), which limits the collapse risk in the near term — but the sub-side signals warrant monitoring.

Stable — no active warning signals

Detroit, Pittsburgh, Louisville, Austin — all clean

None of the four improving or stable markets show the dual-decline pattern. Detroit has held above 78% for 26 months with consistent GC posting above 17%. Pittsburgh and Louisville are recovering. Austin is the most fragile of the four at 78% but GC posting is steady at 18% and active sub % is rising.
