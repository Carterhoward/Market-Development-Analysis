◆ Anomalies & outliers — what the data reveals that isn't obvious
▲ Small-ticket niche punches above its weight — Service Station / Car Wash and Convenience Store GCs ($500K–$2M projects) post 3–5× per month and generate 8–12 bids per project consistently. Low per-project engagement masks high LTV (lifetime value) due to posting frequency and sub loyalty. These are sleeper ICP accounts worth prioritizing in renewals.
▲ Fitness Center overperforms its size tier — Projects valued $1M–$5M generate disproportionately high bid counts due to MEP + specialty finishes + equipment overlap. GCs specializing here attract a premium sub base. This is an undervalued targeting signal in current segmentation.
▸ Military is a feast-or-famine vertical — Peaked at 3.8% of all projects in Aug 2025 (266 projects), collapsed to 0.7% by October. Engagement is high when active but contract cycles are unpredictable and government-driven. Treat as secondary-only — not a targeting anchor.
▼ Civil / Road / Infrastructure GCs are retention risks — Road/Highway peaked at 7.8% in Jan 2025 then dropped below 0.5% by Q3. These GCs post seasonally and produce thin sub engagement when they do. Avoid targeting civil-only portfolios as primary ICP accounts. They inflate acquisition numbers and depress retention metrics.
▼ The $25M+ engagement trap — Large civil and infrastructure projects show extremely high views and downloads but bid submission rates collapse. Subs pull specs and walk away due to bonding and prequalification barriers. Scoring GCs by views or downloads without normalizing for actual bids will systematically overcount the value of these accounts.
◆ October is the platform's peak bidding month — Sub bids reached 139,765 in Oct 2025, up 63% from the Feb 2025 trough of 85,750. Sales onboarding and GC activation efforts must be concentrated in Q3 to ensure new accounts are live and posting before the fall surge window opens.
◆ Key takeaways — what this means for sales and marketing
1 Your ICP is a $2M–$10M commercial GC in Retail, Education, or Municipal. These three verticals account for 41% of all platform volume, attract 14–22 bids per project (45–80% above baseline), and post consistently year-round. This is your highest-confidence sales targeting segment — the foundation of any scoring model.
2 Municipal is the fastest-growing opportunity on the platform right now. Its share of monthly projects surged 123% from Jan 2025 to Jan 2026. GCs entering public works and civic construction are a time-sensitive first-mover acquisition target before competitors recognize the structural shift.
3 Don't be fooled by high views and downloads on large civil projects. A project with 500 plan downloads and 3 bids is underperforming — not overperforming. Normalize all engagement scoring for bids as the primary signal, with downloads and views as secondary confirmation only. Failing to do this systematically misvalues your civil-heavy accounts.
4 The small-ticket niche is a hidden LTV champion. Service Station, Convenience Store, and Car Wash GCs may not look impressive project-by-project, but their posting frequency, sub loyalty, and low churn make them high-value long-term accounts. Prioritize them in renewal and expansion motions.
5 Fix dormancy before scaling acquisition. 49.2% of GCs are inactive. The highest-ROI move right now is reactivating ICP-matching dormant GCs — those with commercial vertical portfolios in the $2M–$25M range who have gone quiet in the last 6 months — before spending on new logo acquisition.
